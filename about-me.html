Dr.-Ing. M. Mekonen

I am a mechanical engineer with a focus on computational methods and material mechanics. My interests include finite element simulation, material modeling, and machine learning applications in engineering. I hold a doctorate (Dr.-Ing.) in engineering, and my research has spanned across academia and applied industrial domains.

I am passionate about interdisciplinary innovation—bringing together physics-based modeling, data-driven methods, and engineering practice in areas like automotive and aerospace.

Research Contributions

As part of my doctoral research at the Fraunhofer IWM and TU Bergakademie Freiberg, I worked extensively on magnesium alloy sheet forming—a crucial topic in lightweight design for mobility sectors.

  • Dissertation: Experimental and Numerical Investigation of Mg Alloy Sheet Forming

Selected Publications

  • Experimental and Numerical Investigation of Mg Alloy Sheet Formability – M. Nebebe Mekonen et al.
  • Mechanical Characterisation of Mg Alloys and Model Parameter Identification for Sheet Forming Simulations – M. Nebebe et al.
